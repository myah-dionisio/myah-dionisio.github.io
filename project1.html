It all started with cutting rectangles out of cardboard, having limited competence in woodwork. As I fiddled with the pieces, slotting them together, I noticed how the forms began to resemble modern architecture through their clean, geometric structures. I translated my inexperience into the work itself - letting minimal technique shape the construction process, with only slot joints connecting each and every component.
The lamp plays on geometry and symmetry, which achieves a sense of cohesion when viewed at multiple angles. Its rich colour and satin-lustre finish conveys luxury and sophistication, with the warm reddish-brown hues of sapele accompanied by the yellow-orange lighting creating a refined look compared to the casual appearance of light woods. In addition, the sapele provides a striking contrast against lighter backgrounds enhancing the overall ambience and elegance of the room. Designed for ease of use with a housing joint connecting the base and frame, the frame can be effortlessly lifted from the base to replace the lightbulb or displayed independently as a sculptural accent in your living space.

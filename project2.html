My lifelong goal has been to continually challenge and expand my skills in garment construction, and the major design project at ACU provided the perfect opportunity to do so. Tailoring was a technique I had not previously explored in my studies, yet I was deeply inspired by the structured silhouettes of fashion houses I admire - such as Maison Margiela and Alexander McQueen - and by their historical foundation: the traditional men’s suit that has evolved over centuries. This form of craftsmanship appealed to me particularly because of its rarity in womenswear.
I wanted to explore the structural integrity of a traditional suit by practicing intricate hand-sewn techniques and revealing them internally. The dress is made from 100% wool sourced from thrifted suit jackets and fabric store off-cuts, acknowledging the natural fabric selections that are now unfavoured, in modern clothing. I deliberately incorporated a synthetic fabric throughout the dress as a visual and textural accent that also symbolises how contemporary garment construction now prioritises lower quality and mass production. The underskirt, also in synthetic fabric, is intentionally left visible to emphasise this contrast and reinforce the overall concept.

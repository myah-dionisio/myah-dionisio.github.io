TEACHING PHILOSOPHY

Creativity has always been at the centre of my practice,
and I see teaching as a way to share this passion while empowering students to develop their own voices as designers and problem-solvers.

I aim to be the kind of educator who uplifts, challenges and nurtures student creativity in meaningful ways. As a Design and Technologies teacher, I apply Universal Design for Learning to create inclusive classrooms where all students can access the curriculum in ways that suit their strengths. Specifically, I offer students choice in how they demonstrate understanding – whether through sketches, prototypes or written reflections.

I use project-based learning to mirror real-world design processes,
encouraging learners to take ownership of their work and learn from re-learning. Through this, I strive to cultivate adaptive thinkers who can connect their ideas to authentic contexts.

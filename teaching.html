My Teaching Philosophy

My Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is shaped by my background in fashion design and my journey into education. Creativity has always been at the centre of my practice, and I see teaching as a way to share this passion while empowering students to develop their own voices as designers and problem-solvers.

I aim to be the kind of educator who uplifts, challenges and nurtures student creativity in meaningful ways. As a Design and Technologies teacher, I apply Universal Design for Learning to create inclusive classrooms where all students can access the curriculum in ways that suit their strengths. Specifically, I offer students choice in how they demonstrate understanding - whether through sketches, prototypes or written reflections. My approach is also informed by constructivist principles, where students build knowledge through inquiry, hands-on projects and iterative design thinking. I use project-based learning to mirror real-world design processes, encouraging learners to take ownership of their work and learn from re-learning. Through this, I strive to cultivate adaptive thinkers who can connect their ideas to authentic contexts. Across my exerience in both primary and secondary education, I have learned that flexibility and strong relationships are key to meaningful learning.
